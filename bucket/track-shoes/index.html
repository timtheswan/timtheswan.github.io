Your Privacy and Cookie Preferences

Your Privacy
The California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA”) provides California consumers the right to opt out of the sale of their personal information. We do not sell personal contact information including name, email, mailing address, or phone number. However, our website does contain digital trackers, including cookies, from third-party advertising partners. These digital trackers are placed on your device and collect information regarding website visits and browsing behavior. This type of data collection is considered a “sale” under the CCPA. Because the digital trackers are located on the device used to access our website, only the user of the device has the ability to turn off the trackers by changing their cookie preferences to set these trackers to “inactive”. Because we do not sell any other categories of personal data, once you turn off these trackers, there is no other action needed.

To learn more about how your data is shared, please see New Balance’s Privacy Policy

Cookie Preferences
The CCPA states that California consumers have the right to opt out of the sale of their personal information. The only way to exercise that right is by turning off third-party advertising trackers (“Targeting Cookies”) by clicking on that section in the menu and setting the toggle to “inactive”. Because we do not sell any other categories of personal data, once you turn off these trackers, there is no other action needed. In addition, you can view other cookie categories and change default settings by clicking on the different category headings. Note that blocking some types of cookies may impact your experience on the site and the services we are able to offer.

Strictly Necessary Cookies

Always Active

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in or filling in forms. You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the site will not then work. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information.

Performance Cookies

Active

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. We also use this information to help improve our website, make our marketing more relevant and improve your experience. The data used for these cookies is aggregated and anonymised, which means that we can’t identify you as an individual. In other words, your personal data is not used for these cookies.

Targeting Cookies

Active

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant ads on other sites. The only way to opt out of the sale of this data is by using the toggle to set these trackers to “inactive”. They do not store directly personal information, but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. Because we do not sell any other categories of personal data, once you turn off these trackers, there is no other action needed.
