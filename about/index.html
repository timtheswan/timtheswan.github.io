Why Computer Science?

Studying Computer science not only gives you a better understanding of the technology that drives our world today, it equips you with the problem solving skills to create that technology.

The remarkable precision and speed with which computers can perform tasks is only possible through the design of a creative problem solver. Computer Science is not about reading binary code or calculating a bunch of numbers, it’s about figuring out how to get a computer to do that for you.

Computer Science Career Stats

Computer Scientists are in enormous demand, there is a profound deficit in graduates to fill the available positions. Even still, Computer Science is a solid career , in fact a 2018 study labeled Computer Science as the highest paying college major.

A field of diverse styles and interests

Computer Science belongs to no one passion, work style, or personality. CS makes so many different things possible, it's difficult to find a passion that technology isn't relevant for. Whether it's making that app you've been dreaming up, helping missions serve as effectively as possible, or improving people's lives, CS is needed.

Computer Scientists work in a wide variety of environments. Whether you prefer to work collaboratively or by yourself, if you want consistency in your job or to shake things up, or a job that requires you to think logically, creatively, or both, there are myriads of strong career choices for you.
